<p><p>The semiconductor valve market comprises various types, including diaphragm valves, bellows valves, vacuum valves, and ball valves. Diaphragm valves are known for their leak-free operation, ideal for corrosive fluids. Bellows valves offer excellent containment and durability in high-purity applications. Vacuum valves are designed to maintain vacuum integrity in semiconductor processes, while ball valves provide quick shut-off and robust performance. Each valve type plays a crucial role in managing fluid control and ensuring process reliability in semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
