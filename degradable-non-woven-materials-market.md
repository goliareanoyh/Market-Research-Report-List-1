<p><p>The degradable non-woven materials market encompasses various types, including spunlaced, melt-blown, spunbond, and needle-punched nonwovens. Spunlaced nonwovens are created by entangling fibers using high-pressure water jets, offering softness and flexibility. Melt-blown nonwovens are formed by extruding molten polymer through fine nozzles, resulting in enhanced filtration properties. Spunbond nonwovens involve extruding fibers onto a conveyor, creating strong, lightweight materials. Needle-punched nonwovens use barbed needles to interlock fibers, providing durability. Together, these materials cater to eco-friendly applications across multiple sectors.</p></p>
